Mastodon

September 20, 2017

Only people who know me personally can tell how much I talk about Mastodon. In case you hadn’t heard of it yet, it’s a microblogging social network (one of those where users share through short posts, 500 characters in Mastodon’s case).

One of the most interesting aspects of the platform is that there’s a close attention being payed by the administrators when it comes to violation of the rules — which means that you as a user of the social media may feel much more safe, once it’s quite easy for the admins to ban bullies or any other kind of aggressor.

There are thounsands of people using it already, many from Japan, followed by France and other nationalities. The other nationalities happen to compose the English posts — the ones I can understand .

As I have been there for some months now, I feel like I’m qualified to say that, Mastodon offers a completely new experience, if you take the fact of it not customizing your feed in order to show ads to you in consideration; time is the only entity to control how the toots will be displayed, which order they’ll follow.

After the very basics

Instances

Now you must learn that Mastodon isn’t a social media on its own. Let me explain it. Everyone can have what they call an “instance“. An instance consists of an installation of Mastodon in a server — you could start your own community, all you’d have to do is install Mastodon somewhere. As the projects has been out there for years now, there are some dozens of instances, like mstdn.io, mastodon.social, mastodon.rocks and many more (like many many more). In that list you can choose one that is closer to your ideal community.
